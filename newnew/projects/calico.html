Overview

Calico is a seed startup that raised 2 million to build a product lifecycle management platform for fashion companies. Their software helps design-focused brands manage everything from sourcing materials, collaborating with factories and tracking on-going orders.

I joined Calico as the first product designer where I led product and design initiatives from discovery to development.

Challenge

  • Empower brands scale from one person to a small product team
  • Improve sustainability efforts in the fashion industry
People problem Fashion product teams feel unorganized and stressed as production process becomes hard to understand. Often times, decisions can be made without product specs being updated.
How do physical products get made?

Most production processes will follow a typical process where they will: design a concept, find a factory, create samples, produce their product then plan shipping logistics. A typical process, may look like this.

How this actually looks like?

From talking to industry experts and researching product development online, we learned that product developer’s responsibilities are designing concepts, selecting materials, providing build specs and collaborating with manufacturers before a product goes to market. A product developer’s workspace will have many materials and prototypes as they prefer to work physically. They will use software such as:

  1. Illustrator/CAD for spec-ing out their products
  2. Asana or Notion for keeping track of workflow
  3. Excel spreadsheets to map out measurements

When a product is being manufactured, you’ll have over 50+ people putting together the product meaning your product development needs to be clearly documented.

Why adopting a PLM can be diffcult

We distilled our learnings into specific pain points on why fashion companies hesitate to adopt a PLM:

Slows down production

There are two main reasons: Most products that are built are often legacy products meaning different teams are using the same product. When a team customizes their inputs, it affects every other team. Also, often times inputting this information is very time-consuming due to the complex user experience.

Locked inside the platform

Product lifecycle software are often designed to retain users by making it hard to migrate data to a new product. However, many manufacturers may need an exported spreadsheets, such as sizing tables or material breakdowns, in order to produce the desired product. Most fashion brands will keep their data on spreadsheets to avoid situations where their business will run on a legacy platform.

Production processes aren’t linear

Many product companies will have different ways of building products such as customizing an existing design pattern provided by a manufacturer to building products from scratch. Current PLMs don’t support tracking feedback and files from factories and stakeholders in a consumable way. For example, many materials could be documented better so different fashion designers can re-use them.

Opportunity We focused on simplifying the fields and bringing complexity where its needed to differientiate the product from existing PLMs.
Creating Products

The first action for a user is to create a product. The purpose of creating a product is so you can track its progression through sampling and production orders. You’ll be able to add any information similar to a Techpack.

Bill of Materials and Material Library

The Bill Of materials (BOM) is used to track materials used in production. This includes materials from base material to packaging. From the BOM, many other functional documents can be made such as a cost breakdown and material checklist.

A material library allows brands can contact previous suppliers and track data for each material.

Product feedback alongside specs

PLMs solve feedback differently. For example, Backbone uses a comment tool only from the brand side. For Calico, we decided to bring in comments for all product related feedback.

In our user interviews, we learned that users would like to move between comments and product specs—so we added a split screen view.

Personal learnings
Set up your foundations

The most important part about building products is setting up foundations. It can be anything from design systems, project scopes to personal goals. By making your life easier in the future, you will feel less burnout and be able to figure out then execute on the highest impact initiatives.

Empower the team

The most important lesson I learned at Calico was to empower the team. Sometimes that may mean doing extra documentation for handoff or being that person to remind the team to keep going even if it is difficult. I learned that the highest impact people can achieve that by helping their team multiply their impact.
